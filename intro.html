Introduction

This study explores air pollution in Malaysia, analyzing key pollutants like PM₁₀, CO, and NOₓ across urban, rural, and industrial regions. It highlights seasonal trends, the impact of transboundary haze, and long-term changes using statistical and trajectory models. Findings reveal significant health and environmental risks, urging data-driven policies, stricter emission controls, and regional collaboration to improve air quality and mitigate impacts on public health.
